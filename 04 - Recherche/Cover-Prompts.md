# Cover-Prompts — Die Ashworth Drei (Buch 1–9)

Alle Prompts verwenden denselben visuellen Stil: hand-drawn ink and watercolor, atmosphärisch-britisch, middle-grade mystery. Kühle blau-graue Grundpalette mit einem zentralen warmen Lichtakzent. Die drei Protagonisten sind auf jedem Cover präsent. Jedes Cover trägt Text in ornamentaler goldener Serifenschrift (Serie, Titel, Band, Autor).

## Wiederkehrende Charakterbeschreibungen

Diese Beschreibungen gelten für alle Prompts. Bei späteren Bänden altern die Figuren leicht (siehe Anmerkungen).

**Finn Calloway (13, ab Buch 4: 14):** Lanky boy, slightly too tall for his age. Messy dark-blond hair falling into his forehead. Alert grey eyes. Wearing an oversized, worn olive-green waxed jacket — clearly an adult's jacket, too big for him, sleeves pushed up at the wrists. A chewed-up pencil sticks out of his jacket pocket.

**Priya Sharma (13, ab Buch 4: 14):** Compact build, energetic posture. Long black hair in a practical ponytail. South Asian features, dark attentive eyes. Jeans and hoodie. Carries a comically oversized backpack stuffed to the brim.

**Archie Wynn (12, ab Buch 4: 13):** Smallest and slightest of the three. Straight light-brown hair falling over his ears. Freckled face, large watchful eyes. Dark nondescript clothes, black Converse sneakers. Holds a battered sketchbook pressed against his chest.

## Buch 1 — Das Licht im Badehaus

Book cover in hand-drawn illustration style — like traditional ink and watercolor children's book illustration. Sketchy visible linework, muted washed colors, hand-painted textures. NOT digital art, NOT 3D rendering, NOT photorealistic. Think classic illustrated middle-grade novel cover with a hand-crafted feel.

Text in ornate golden serif font with warm glow:
- Top center small: DIE ASHWORTH DREI
- Center large ornamental gold with glow: Das Licht im Badehaus
- Small below: Band 1
- Bottom center clean serif: C. J. Whitmore

**Setting:** A misty evening along an old, overgrown canal in a quiet English suburb. In the background looms a derelict Victorian bathhouse — dark red-brown brick, tall arched windows bricked up with lighter stone, ivy crawling up the north wall to the second floor, a collapsed glass roof leaving only a skeleton of steel beams against a deep twilight sky. A faded "Danger — Keep Out" sign hangs on a rusted iron fence, bent and sagging in places. The canal reflects dim light. The atmosphere is eerie but inviting — not horror, but mystery.

**Central image:** A warm, flickering golden light glows faintly through a narrow gap in one of the bricked-up ground-floor windows — the only warm color in an otherwise cool, blue-grey palette. The light suggests a lantern or candle moving inside.

**Characters:** Three teenagers seen from behind or in three-quarter profile, standing at the canal's edge, looking toward the bathhouse. Finn — tallest, lanky, messy dark-blond hair, oversized olive waxed jacket torn and taped near the left elbow — stands in the centre. Priya — compact build, long black ponytail, enormous backpack on her back — stands slightly forward, one hand on a backpack strap, a flashlight shape visible poking from the bag. Archie — smallest and slightest, light-brown hair over his ears, dark clothes, black Converse — stands slightly behind the other two, half in shadow, sketchbook pressed to his chest, eyes fixed on the bathhouse.

**Mood & Palette:** Cool blue-grey tones dominate — mist, dusk, stone, water. The only warm accent is the golden flicker from the bathhouse window, casting a faint glow on the canal water. Anticipation, not fear.

**Composition:** The bathhouse fills the upper half, the three kids occupy the lower third, seen slightly from behind and below, emphasizing the building's looming presence. The canal runs between them and the building. Mist curls low over the water.

## Buch 2 — Der falsche Rahmen

Book cover in hand-drawn illustration style — like traditional ink and watercolor children's book illustration. Sketchy visible linework, muted washed colors, hand-painted textures. NOT digital art, NOT 3D rendering, NOT photorealistic. Think classic illustrated middle-grade novel cover with a hand-crafted feel.

Text in ornate golden serif font with warm glow:
- Top center small: DIE ASHWORTH DREI
- Center large ornamental gold with glow: Der falsche Rahmen
- Small below: Band 2
- Bottom center clean serif: C. J. Whitmore

**Setting:** A grand but slightly shabby village hall interior during a charity auction. Long wooden tables display framed paintings, porcelain, and bric-a-brac. Warm overhead lights cast pools of amber on the items. In the background, a crowd of adults in coats and scarves mill about. Through tall windows, the grey rooftops of a Bristol suburb are visible in fading afternoon light.

**Central image:** An ornate gilded picture frame sits prominently on an easel at the front of the hall. The painting inside shows a country house that no longer exists — a stately manor surrounded by parkland, painted in warm oils. The frame itself glows with a subtle golden light, almost too perfect for its surroundings. One corner of the frame shows a faint, almost hidden marking — a small engraved symbol.

**Characters:** The three teenagers are positioned in the mid-ground, partly obscured by the crowd, clearly not fitting in among the adults. Finn — tallest, lanky, messy dark-blond hair, oversized olive waxed jacket — leans forward, grey eyes locked on the painting, pencil between his teeth. Priya — compact build, long black ponytail, enormous backpack on her back — is on her toes, peering over someone's shoulder, one hand steadying her bag. Archie — smallest and slightest, light-brown hair over his ears, dark clothes, black Converse — crouches slightly, sketchbook open, rapidly drawing the painting from memory. They exchange a glance — they've noticed something no one else has.

**Mood & Palette:** Warm amber interior light contrasts with the cold grey visible through the windows. The painting glows like a window into a lost world. The rest of the hall feels slightly sepia-toned, crowded, mundane — making the painting stand out as the one extraordinary thing in the room.

**Composition:** The painting on its easel dominates the upper centre. The three kids form a diagonal line in the lower half, threading through the crowd. Depth of field effect: the adults are slightly blurred, the kids and the painting sharp.

## Buch 3 — Die Kapsel in der Mauer

Book cover in hand-drawn illustration style — like traditional ink and watercolor children's book illustration. Sketchy visible linework, muted washed colors, hand-painted textures. NOT digital art, NOT 3D rendering, NOT photorealistic. Think classic illustrated middle-grade novel cover with a hand-crafted feel.

Text in ornate golden serif font with warm glow:
- Top center small: DIE ASHWORTH DREI
- Center large ornamental gold with glow: Die Kapsel in der Mauer
- Small below: Band 3
- Bottom center clean serif: C. J. Whitmore

**Setting:** A weathered red-brick school building, clearly Victorian-era, with tall arched windows and a slate roof. A section of the exterior wall has been partially demolished — scaffolding, rubble, exposed brickwork. It's an overcast afternoon, the sky heavy and silver-grey. A few autumn leaves drift across the empty playground.

**Central image:** In the broken wall, a dark rectangular cavity is visible — the space where a time capsule used to be. A few loose bricks lie on the ground below it. The cavity is empty. A faint rectangular dust-outline shows something was recently removed. One thin cobweb stretches across the gap, freshly broken.

**Characters:** The three stand at the base of the scaffolding, looking up at the empty cavity. Finn —  lanky, messy dark-blond hair, oversized olive waxed jacket — has one hand on a scaffolding pole, his expression intense. Priya — long black ponytail, jeans and reddish hoodie, enormous backpack on her back — holds a newspaper clipping, comparing something in the article to what they see. Archie — smallest and slightest, light-brown hair over his ears, dark clothes, black Converse — is already sketching the cavity and the surrounding brickwork, capturing details others would miss. The school looms behind them, institutional and secretive.

**Mood & Palette:** Cool grey-silver sky, warm red brick, the dark void of the empty cavity as a focal black point. Autumnal — scattered leaves, bare branches visible at the edges. The mood is unsettling: something was here, and someone took it. First hint of a larger pattern.

**Composition:** The school wall fills the upper two-thirds, with the dark cavity slightly off-centre as the focal point. The three kids are at the bottom, small against the building, emphasizing scale and the weight of institutional secrets.

## Buch 4 — Tiefgang

Book cover in hand-drawn illustration style — like traditional ink and watercolor children's book illustration. Sketchy visible linework, muted washed colors, hand-painted textures. NOT digital art, NOT 3D rendering, NOT photorealistic. Think classic illustrated middle-grade novel cover with a hand-crafted feel.

Text in ornate golden serif font with warm glow:
- Top center small: DIE ASHWORTH DREI
- Center large ornamental gold with glow: Tiefgang
- Small below: Band 4
- Bottom center clean serif: C. J. Whitmore

*(Characters are now slightly older: Finn & Priya 14, Archie 13.)*

**Setting:** The mouth of an old canal tunnel, where a quiet suburban waterway meets Bristol's harbour district. The tunnel entrance is a dark stone arch, half-submerged, draped with moss and lichen. Beyond the arch, the tunnel disappears into blackness. On one side, the overgrown towpath of Ashworth Green. On the other, just visible through the tunnel, the distant lights and cranes of Bristol's docklands glow faintly orange.

**Central image:** The tunnel mouth itself — a perfect dark arch reflected in still, black water, creating an oval of darkness. From deep inside the tunnel, a single faint light reflects off the water's surface, suggesting something (or someone) far within. Old iron mooring rings are embedded in the stone. A frayed rope trails into the water.

**Characters:** The three are on the towpath at the tunnel's edge, clearly debating whether to go in. Finn — tallest, lanky, messy dark-blond hair, oversized olive waxed jacket — stands closest to the water, leaning forward, one foot on a mooring stone. Priya — compact build, long black ponytail, enormous backpack open — pulls out a proper torch; she came prepared. Archie — smallest and slightest, light-brown hair over his ears, dark clothes, black Converse — sits on the canal bank, sketchbook on his knees, drawing the tunnel entrance, but his eyes look past his drawing into the darkness. They're older now — slightly taller, more confident in their postures, but the darkness ahead is new.

**Mood & Palette:** The darkest cover in the series so far. Deep blacks and blue-greys dominate, with faint warm light from Bristol's harbour in the far background. The water is mirror-still and black. The tunnel mouth is the visual anchor — a portal between their safe suburban world and something bigger. Tension, not adventure.

**Composition:** The tunnel arch dominates the centre, perfectly framed. The three kids are to the left on the towpath, small against the massive stone entrance. Bristol's distant lights are a faint glow through the tunnel on the right. The reflection in the water doubles the arch into a dark oval.

## Buch 5 — Absender unbekannt

Book cover in hand-drawn illustration style — like traditional ink and watercolor children's book illustration. Sketchy visible linework, muted washed colors, hand-painted textures. NOT digital art, NOT 3D rendering, NOT photorealistic. Think classic illustrated middle-grade novel cover with a hand-crafted feel.

Text in ornate golden serif font with warm glow:
- Top center small: DIE ASHWORTH DREI
- Center large ornamental gold with glow: Absender unbekannt
- Small below: Band 5
- Bottom center clean serif: C. J. Whitmore

**Setting:** A row of terraced houses on a quiet English suburban street — red brick, small front gardens, wheelie bins, a postbox on the corner. It's early morning, pre-dawn, the sky a gradient from dark indigo to pale grey. Streetlamps cast amber pools on wet pavement. Every house is dark except for a few early-morning kitchen lights glowing behind curtains.

**Central image:** A cluster of envelopes scattered across a doorstep, as if pushed through a letterbox in haste. The envelopes are plain white, handwritten addresses in old-fashioned script, no return address. One envelope has been opened — the letter inside is partially visible, showing dense handwriting. A second envelope lies in a puddle on the garden path, the ink beginning to bleed. The letters seem to glow faintly white against the dark stone step.

**Characters:** The three are further down the street, half-hidden behind a garden wall, observing the doorstep. Finn — tallest, lanky, messy dark-blond hair, oversized olive waxed jacket with collar turned up against the cold — peers over the wall, grey eyes narrowed. Priya — compact build, long black ponytail, enormous backpack on her back — crouches beside him, holding a list, cross-referencing addresses. Archie — smallest and slightest, light-brown hair over his ears, dark clothes, black Converse — is pressed flat against the wall, nearly invisible, watching not the door but the street behind them, making sure they're not followed. A figure is dimly visible in an upstairs window of a house further down the street — someone else is awake, watching.

**Mood & Palette:** Pre-dawn indigo and grey, with warm amber streetlamp pools. The white envelopes are the brightest element — almost luminous against the dark. The mood is secretive, conspiratorial. Suburbia feels unfamiliar in the half-dark — familiar houses made strange by shadows.

**Composition:** The street recedes into perspective, drawing the eye down the row of houses. The scattered envelopes on the doorstep are in sharp focus in the foreground. The three kids are in the mid-ground, partially hidden behind the wall. The watching figure in the distant window is barely perceptible — a silhouette detail that rewards a second look.

## Buch 6 — Das letzte Schloss

Book cover in hand-drawn illustration style — like traditional ink and watercolor children's book illustration. Sketchy visible linework, muted washed colors, hand-painted textures. NOT digital art, NOT 3D rendering, NOT photorealistic. Think classic illustrated middle-grade novel cover with a hand-crafted feel.

Text in ornate golden serif font with warm glow:
- Top center small: DIE ASHWORTH DREI
- Center large ornamental gold with glow: Das letzte Schloss
- Small below: Band 6
- Bottom center clean serif: C. J. Whitmore

**Setting:** The interior of a small, cluttered cottage — Grandpa Ted's home. A worn armchair by a fireplace, shelves overflowing with old books and tins, a workbench covered in locks, keys, and tools. Late afternoon light slants through a small window, catching dust motes. The room is warm but feels fragile — as if preserved in amber.

**Central image:** On the workbench, in a pool of golden afternoon light, sits an old, ornate padlock — unlike Ted's usual utilitarian locks. It's brass, tarnished green with age, engraved with a pattern that echoes the magpie motif. Beside it lies an old photograph, face-down, and a folded piece of paper with faded handwriting. Ted's knotty hands rest on the bench edge, as if he's just set these things down — or is about to take them away. His hands are the emotional centre: scarred, oil-stained, strong but trembling slightly.

**Characters:** Archie — smallest and slightest, light-brown hair over his ears, dark clothes, black Converse — stands in the doorway, backlit by grey daylight from outside, his face in shadow but his posture readable — frozen mid-step, as if he's walked in on something he wasn't meant to see. His sketchbook hangs loose at his side. Through the doorway behind him, on the canal path, Finn and Priya wait — Finn (tallest, lanky, messy dark-blond hair, oversized olive waxed jacket) with his hands in his jacket pockets, looking concerned; Priya (compact build, long black ponytail, enormous backpack) with her hand raised, about to knock on a door that's already open.

**Mood & Palette:** The warmest cover — amber, gold, deep brown. But the warmth feels autumnal, fading. The cottage interior glows like the last embers of a fire. Outside (visible through the door) is cold grey. The contrast between warm interior and cold exterior mirrors the story: what Ted has built is fragile, and time is running out.

**Composition:** The workbench with the lock and Ted's hands fills the lower half (Ted's face is not fully visible — just hands and the suggestion of a figure in the armchair). Archie in the doorway occupies the centre, the threshold between inside and outside. Finn and Priya are visible through the doorway in the background. Layers of depth: bench → Archie → friends → the grey world outside.

Text in ornate golden serif font with warm glow:
- Top center small: DIE ASHWORTH DREI
- Center large ornamental gold with glow: Die Spur der Elstern
- Small below: Band 7
- Bottom center clean serif: C. J. Whitmore

*(Characters are now older: Finn & Priya 15, Archie 14.)*

**Setting:** Multiple locations in Ashworth Green shown simultaneously in a dreamlike, layered composition — as if the suburb has been unfolded like a map. The canal, the high street, the school, the churchyard, garden walls — all visible in a bird's-eye, slightly tilted perspective. It's a grey, drizzly day. The suburb looks ordinary, mundane, soaked in rain.

**Central image:** Scattered across the scene, on walls, fence posts, drain covers, and carved into tree bark, small magpie symbols are visible — stylized birds in black and white, some painted, some scratched, some chalked. They form a pattern across the landscape, connecting different locations like dots on a map. The magpie symbols are the warm accent: each one glints slightly gold, as if catching light from a source that isn't visible. Viewed together, they trace a path through the suburb — a treasure trail left by someone who knows the place intimately.

**Characters:** The three are positioned at different points across the unfolded landscape, each examining a different magpie symbol. Finn — tallest, lanky, messy dark-blond hair, oversized olive waxed jacket — stands at a garden wall, tracing a painted magpie with his finger. Priya — compact build, long black ponytail, enormous backpack — is at the canal bridge, photographing a carved symbol on the stone with her phone. Archie — smallest and slightest, light-brown hair over his ears, dark clothes, black Converse — crouches by a drain cover near the school, sketching the pattern into his book. Despite being separated in the image, invisible dotted lines connect them — they're mapping the same mystery from different angles.

**Mood & Palette:** Grey-green suburban drizzle as the base. The magpie symbols punctuate the scene with flashes of gold-black-white — the only vivid elements in an otherwise muted landscape. The mood is puzzle-like, obsessive — like staring at a hidden-picture illustration until the pattern emerges.

**Composition:** Slightly elevated, tilted bird's-eye view of the suburb, as if drawn on an unfolded map. The three kids at different points create a triangle. The magpie symbols form a winding path through the scene. The viewer's eye follows the trail, connecting the dots.

## Buch 8 — Das verschwundene Archiv

Book cover in hand-drawn illustration style — like traditional ink and watercolor children's book illustration. Sketchy visible linework, muted washed colors, hand-painted textures. NOT digital art, NOT 3D rendering, NOT photorealistic. Think classic illustrated middle-grade novel cover with a hand-crafted feel.

Text in ornate golden serif font with warm glow:
- Top center small: DIE ASHWORTH DREI
- Center large ornamental gold with glow: Das verschwundene Archiv
- Small below: Band 8
- Bottom center clean serif: C. J. Whitmore

**Setting:** A large, shadowy room — possibly a cellar, a disused archive, or a hidden storage space beneath an old building. Metal shelving units line the walls, stretching into darkness. The shelves are mostly empty — conspicuously, recently empty. Dust outlines show where boxes, folders, and objects once stood. A few items remain: a single cardboard box with its lid askew, a scattered handful of old photographs on the floor, a broken filing cabinet with one drawer pulled out and emptied.

**Central image:** The empty shelves themselves are the subject — the negative space, the absence. Where a collection once filled every surface, now there are only ghosts: dust rectangles, faded labels, a single forgotten document caught under a shelf leg. One bare lightbulb hangs from the ceiling, swinging slightly, casting moving shadows. The light is harsh and white — industrial, not warm. Someone cleared this room in a hurry, and recently.

**Characters:** The three stand in the middle of the emptied room, surrounded by absence. Finn — tallest, lanky, messy dark-blond hair, oversized olive waxed jacket — picks up a photograph from the floor, holding it to the light, his expression grim; they were too late. Priya — compact build, long black ponytail, enormous backpack still on her back — stands at the broken filing cabinet, pulling out the single remaining folder, her jaw set. Archie — smallest and slightest, light-brown hair over his ears, dark clothes, black Converse — is on his knees, running his hand over dust outlines on a low shelf, reading the shapes of what was taken. His sketchbook is open on the floor beside him — he's already drawing the empty spaces, documenting what's gone. The mood between them is tense but determined.

**Mood & Palette:** The coldest, starkest cover. Harsh white artificial light against deep shadow. No warmth. Steel grey, concrete, dust. The emptiness is the point — a visual gut-punch. This is what it looks like when someone erases history. The faintest warm accent: the yellowed edge of the single remaining photograph in Finn's hand.

**Composition:** The empty shelves recede into shadow in the upper half, creating depth and unease. The three kids are central, small in the large empty space. The swinging bulb casts dynamic shadows. A single door at the far end of the room stands open — whoever did this left in a hurry.

## Buch 9 — Der Sammler

Book cover in hand-drawn illustration style — like traditional ink and watercolor children's book illustration. Sketchy visible linework, muted washed colors, hand-painted textures. NOT digital art, NOT 3D rendering, NOT photorealistic. Think classic illustrated middle-grade novel cover with a hand-crafted feel. Designed to echo and resolve the visual language of all previous books in the series.

Text in ornate golden serif font with warm glow:
- Top center small: DIE ASHWORTH DREI
- Center large ornamental gold with glow: Der Sammler
- Small below: Band 9
- Bottom center clean serif: C. J. Whitmore

*(Characters are now older: Finn & Priya 16, Archie 15.)*

**Setting:** The rooftop of The Magpie at dusk, moored at its usual spot on the dead end of the old canal — the same location as the final image of Book 1, but transformed. Ashworth Green spreads out behind them — the bathhouse, the school, the high street, the church spire, the cottage, all visible in the fading light. The suburb is rendered in warm golden-hour tones for the first time in the series — not grey, not misty, but bathed in the last light of a long summer day. The canal reflects the sky in golds and pinks.

**Central image:** On the narrowboat's roof, a collection of objects is spread out — the accumulated evidence of nine books: photographs, old documents, a tarnished brass padlock with a magpie engraving, newspaper clippings, sketches torn from Archie's book, a painted magpie symbol on a loose brick, the gilded corner of a picture frame. It's a miniature version of the Sammler's collection — but this one is theirs, assembled by three kids over years of investigation. The objects catch the golden light, each one a callback to a previous book.

**Characters:** The three sit on the roof of The Magpie, older now — taller, more angular, no longer children but not yet adults. Finn — tallest, lanky, messy dark-blond hair, oversized olive waxed jacket still too big but fitting better now — sits with his legs dangling over the side, looking out over the suburb with an expression that is no longer restless but resolved. Priya — compact build, long black ponytail, hoodie — sits cross-legged, her legendary enormous backpack finally set down beside her, open, half-empty for once. She holds a single document, reading it. Archie — smallest and slightest, light-brown hair over his ears, dark clothes, black Converse — leans against the cabin roof, sketchbook open to a page we can almost see — a map of Ashworth Green with every secret location marked. For the first time in the series, he's not hiding behind the other two. He sits between them, equal, present. They're together, at ease, and they know something the reader is about to learn.

**Mood & Palette:** The only warm-palette cover in the series. Golden hour light, amber, soft orange, deep pink sky fading to indigo at the top. The canal glows. Ashworth Green — always grey and misty before — is revealed as beautiful in the right light. The mood is resolution, earned warmth, quiet triumph. Not a cliffhanger — a homecoming.

**Composition:** The three on the boat roof occupy the lower half, seen from slightly above and behind — an echo of Book 1's composition, but reversed: in Book 1 they looked toward the unknown bathhouse; here they look back over the whole suburb, everything they've uncovered. The skyline of Ashworth Green fills the upper half, backlit by sunset. The objects on the boat roof create a still-life border between the kids and the view. The faded magpie painting on the boat's bow is just visible at the bottom edge — the final detail.
